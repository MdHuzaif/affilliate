Best TRX40 Motherboard for AMD Ryzen Threadripper 3960X, 3970X and 3990X

Published on : June 22,2021 by IstiakAhmed

Threadripper aimed at the smallest portion of the computer market but it also the most exciting showcase of the craziness AMD can do. Threadripper allows an unprecedented amount of high core count and every generation sees a double of it. We see 16 cores with threadripper first series then we see 32 cores with threadripper second generation and now 64 cores and 128 threads on latest 3rd generation threadripper. To really harness all the potential of that processor you need the right kind of motherboard.

There are 3 processors available in AMD Ryzen Threadripper 3rd Generation. These are Ryzen Threadripper 3960X with 24 cores and 48 Threads, Ryzen Threadripper 3970X with 32 cores and 64 threads, and Ryzen Threadripper 3990X with massive 64 cores and 128 threads. Threadripper 3rd gen motherboards contain TRX40 socket for 3rd gen CPUs. TRX40 socket doesn’t support 1st or 2nd gen Threadripper. So, these TRX40 motherboards only support 3rd gen threadripper CPUs and above.

Threadripper 3rd Gen processor combined with TRX40 will give you about 88 latest PCIe 4.0 Lanes. This will give you more or less 170 Gigabytes of bandwidth. This is unprecedented and potentially a game-changer in the workstation world. The main reason for the TRX40 motherboard’s high price is the PCIe 4.0 as they don’t come cheap. 3rd Generation threadripper CPUs will come with up to 64 cores and 128 threads and obviously to even hope power that many cores, the manufacturers had to deploy an unprecedented amount of power. TRX40 chipset can supports up to 20 x 3.2 Gen 2 with 10 Gigabit plugs.

If you are a gamer you absolutely don’t need to upgrade your PC to threadripper. Because having high core counts actually will negatively impact your gaming performances. You will have to use Ryzen Master to deactivate some cores to increase your gaming performance. For workstations like video rendering and video editors, the Threadripper platform is perfect for you. Here are our selections of best motherboards for 3rd Generation AMD Ryzen Threadripper CPUs 3990X, 3970X, 3960X.

ASUS ROG Zenith ll Extreme is a TRX40 motherboard so it won’t work with first or second gen threadripper CPUs rather it will work on the latest 3rd Gen 24 and 32 core threadripper CPUs. This motherboard is a monster board. This Threadripper 3960X motherboard has a built-in 1.7-inch OLED display which is what your postcodes come upon. 8 memory slots, 4 memory channels, 4 PCI Express by 16 slots. Many people search asking “best motherboard for threadripper 3990X” or “Best 3970X Threadripper motherboard” well this utter monster motherboard from ASUS is certainly one of the best answers.

There are tons of USB connectivity on this Threadripper 3rd gen motherboard, it has dual Type-C front panel connections in addition to a 30 pin USB connection for the front and a ton of SATA. At the top of the mobo, there is a massive VRM heatsink. This motherboard doesn’t have phase doublers it’s something Asus refers to as a teaming VRM solution. These TRX CPUs will consume 280 watts out of the box That’s TDP 280 watts. The VRMs can get a little toasty. This motherboard has two VRM fans at the top and a heat pipe connection to the rear I/O so this TRX40 motherboard does a lot in the way of heat dissipation.

There is also a chipset fan. There is a total 3 fan on this 3960X motherboard. Some people complain about those fans making a little bit of a whining noise. This motherboard also has inputs for water block stuff. In addition to having some analog temperature sensors in the box that you can plug into the motherboard. ASUS has really done a lot of cool stuff to integrate fan sensors and fan sensor expansion and similar things in their boards. But they are still no GUI to set the fan curves. Every other motherboard has a nice GUI for managing fans. Generally, TRX40 motherboards generally have 4 slots because PCIe 4.0 is expensive.

M.2 number 3 shares bandwidth with an ASMedia controller. If you want the ASMedia USB ports you will have to give it two of your four lanes in that M.2 number 3. Fortunately, there is M.2 on the back of the motherboard, two M.2 on the front of the motherboard. 5 PCI Express 4 in NVMe drives. Which is crazy. There is StoreMI, you can do NVMe RAID 1, 0, and 10. The armory creates option in the UEFI will allow windows to automatically run an ASUS binary when it first boots up which will install the ASUS armory create. A high-end audio solution is built-in into this motherboard.

Asus Prime TRX40-PRO

ASUS Prime TRX40-Pro is a TRX40 powered motherboard that is the first motherboard coming in a PCIe 4.0 standard. It’s still compatible with PCIe 3rd gen. This 3960X motherboard is a compact ATX 6 layered PCB. It is very good for VRM heat dissipation. This motherboard has 1960 amps power stages and 16 of which are dedicated to the CPU power delivery. This means 960 amps almost obscene kiloamps to run and overclock 3960X or 3970X threadripper CPUs. With that much power comes great heat signatures and this is precisely why ASUS has designed that massive space-looking five-centimeter tool heatsink. This will be enough to keep your VRM cool when running and even overclocking the threadripper 3970X 32 core processor. This is a workstation processor and motherboard with custom water cooling and monoblock should be considered.

This Ryzen 3960X motherboard has a quad-channel configuration supporting up to 256 gigabytes of DDR4 Ram overclockable up to 4.4 GHz. This is not surprising because of how much memory you need to have to keep running to feeding 128 threads. Prime TRX40 Pro supports up to three M.2 solid state drives, sticks two horizontal and one vertical. 3rd gen M.2 Solid state drives achieve speed up to 32 Gbps and with PCIe 4.0 enable M.2 SSD you can double that and see massive transfer rate peaking at 64 Gbps. This will produce quite a bit of heat and this is precisely what ASUS came up with a massive mono plate Thermo padded heat shield to keep our horizontal sticks cool as they can be. There is a bit of concern about their placement one because they are right next to each other and second they are trapped right under multiple GPU configurations. If you are planning to boot from M.2 SSD then it is recommended to place it on the vertical mount away from those infernal heat-producing GPUs.

VRM shock phases are placed right next to the TRX40 chipset and have their own dedicated radial heatsink. This is done only to reduce the cost of the motherboard. Three power stages should be placed under the I/O roofing. In this case, ASUS would need to create two block VRM heatsink and connected it by a heat pipe. This will increase the cost way more. Great move by ASUS to make ASUS Prime TRX40 Pro the best cheap motherboard for Threadripper 3960X, 3970X. TRX40 chipset ken supports up to 20 x 3.2 Gen 2 with 10 Gigabit plugs. But sadly this motherboard supports only 5 x USB 3.2 Gen 2 with 10 Gigabit speed with 1 type C. This is also another cost reduction approach from ASUS. Many people look for “best motherboard for threadripper 3960X” or “cheap 3960X motherboard”, well ASUS Prime TRX40 Pro is the best option. There are some more reasons for its lower price.

There is no Wi-Fi adapter on this motherboard this is also another disappointing aspect of the ASUS pro series motherboards. There is an onboard Wi-Fi expansion slot which is an easy and inexpensive Wi-Fi/Bluetooth upgrade. There is also no 10 Gigabit LAN on the back panel this is also a trick by ASUS to manufacture a budget-friendly threadripper 3rd Gen motherboard. ASUS Prime TRX40 Pro is the best motherboard for threadripper 3960X and 3970X but not recommended for 3990X due to lack of some important features to make it cheap.
